## 0. PRE-REGISTER interpretation guards (mandatory)

Every claim below is scoped by the following three guards:

1. **Comparison distribution.** All SDs are to **matched-rate** $\mathrm{LPN}_{p_{\rm eff}(n)}$ with
   $$
   p_{\rm eff}(n)=\frac{1-(3/4)^{2n}}{2},
   $$
   the output's own effective per-coordinate noise rate.  No comparison to $\mathrm{LPN}_{1/4}$ is made.
2. **Scaling axis.** We study **$m$ increasing at fixed $n$** (the correct axis for lem:m2).  No fixed-small-$m$ conclusion is drawn.
3. **Usability of the LPN target.** $p_{\rm eff}(n)\to 1/2$ as $n\to\infty$, so the target itself approaches trivial noise-$1/2$ LPN.  The SD numbers measure whether the **correlation** introduced by marginal-adaptive $B$ is detectable inside a vacuous LPN regime; they do **not** imply a practical distinguisher for standard LPN.

## 1. Exact mixture decomposition (THEOREM, verified)

For the uniform-$B$-per-$A$ reduction with $A\sim\mathrm{Unif}(\mathrm{Lagr}(2n,\mathbb F_2))$, $x\sim\mathrm{Unif}(\mathbb F_2^n)$, $e\sim\mathrm{Bernoulli}(1/4)^{2n}$, and $B\sim\mathrm{Unif}(\mathbb F_2^{m\times 2n})$, the output $(C,y)=(BA,\,B(Ax+e))$ satisfies

$$
P_{\rm out}=q(n)\,P_{\rm graph}+(1-q(n))\,P_{\rm full},
$$

where

$$
q(n)=\Pr[Ax+e\in A]=\Bigl(\frac34\Bigr)^{2n}+\frac{1-(3/4)^{2n}}{2^n+1},
$$

$P_{\rm full}$ is uniform over $\mathbb F_2^{m\times n}\times\mathbb F_2^m$, and $P_{\rm graph}$ is the distribution generated by drawing $C$ uniformly and then drawing $y$ uniformly from the column space of $C$.

**Proof sketch.** Condition on the ambient error $e$:

* If $e=0$ then $v=Ax\in A$ with probability $1$.
* If $e\neq 0$, then $v\in A$ iff $e\in A$.  For any fixed nonzero $e$, the fraction of Lagrangian subspaces containing $e$ is exactly $1/(2^n+1)$ (transitivity of $\mathrm{Sp}(2n,\mathbb F_2)$ on nonzero vectors).

Averaging over $e$ gives $q(n)$.  When $v\in A$, write $v=A c$; the conditional distribution of $c$ is uniform over $\mathbb F_2^n$ (it is $x$ when $e=0$ and $x+c_e$ when $e\in A\setminus\{0\}$).  Hence, conditional on $v\in A$, the output is $(C,C c)$ with $c$ uniform, which is exactly $P_{\rm graph}$.  When $v\notin A$, the linear map $b\mapsto (bA,\,bv)$ has full rank $n+1$, so the image is the full space. ∎

**Verification.** The script checks the mixture SD against the from-scratch integer-count enumeration in `experiments/lib/lem_m2_exact.py` for $n=2$, $m=2,3,4$; all three values match exactly.

### 1.1 Correction (post-adjudication)

The first version of `experiments/200-KIMI-trackA-matched-rate-SD-monotonicity.py`
used an integer-scaling shortcut with floor division (`D // (q_den * size)`).
For $n=3$, $q(3)=1241/4608$ has an odd factor $9$, which does **not** divide
$D/\text{size}$ or $D/(\text{num}_C\cdot2^r)$; the resulting "exact" fractions
were therefore truncated by at most one count per bucket.  The floats were
correct to $\sim$21 digits at $m=6$ but the rational records were wrong.

The script has been fixed to clear the denominator $q_{\rm den}$ exactly:
all comparisons are carried out against denominator $D\cdot q_{\rm den}$,
so no floor division occurs.  The corrected values were independently
re-derived in `experiments/201-KIMI-trackA-mixture-exact-verification.py` and
match Claude's adjudication fractions exactly.  The same fix was applied to
`experiments/191-KIMI-lem-m2-uniform-B-matched-rate-m2n.py` and its
$n=3,m=6$ output.

## 4. Interpretation for lem:m2

* The matched-rate SD **grows** with $m$ at fixed $n$.  This is the opposite of the misleading fixed-small-$m$, $\mathrm{LPN}_{1/4}$ reading from experiment 189.
* As $n$ grows at $m=2n$, the matched SD decreases ($\approx 0.25$ at $n=2$, $\approx 0.22$ at $n=3$).  This is expected because $p_{\rm eff}\to 1/2$ and the LPN target becomes vacuous.
* These computations **do not close** lem:m2.  They only show that the uniform-$B$-per-$A$ marginal-adaptive strategy does not produce a small-SD embedding into a usable LPN instance along the relevant $m$-axis.
