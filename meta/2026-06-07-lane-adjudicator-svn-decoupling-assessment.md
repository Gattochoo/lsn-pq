## 0. The question handed to this track

The handoff localized the LSN worst→avg barrier entirely to the **noise**: instance
randomization is free (Witt: `Sp` transitive on Lagrangians), but the self-dual noise
is rigid (`g(0)=2^{-n}`). The proposed live route is to use the **Weil representation**
to randomize the *instance* while keeping the noise at a usable rate — i.e. to
**decouple code from noise**, the one thing LWE gets for free (code = noise = the
Gaussian) and LSN seemingly cannot. This track adjudicates: **does the Weil/symplectic
structure supply that decoupling, or does Stone–von Neumann rigidity re-impose the
barrier?**

## 1. The decoupling, made precise

"Randomize the code by a symplectic `g` while leaving the noise fixed" is, at the
linear-algebra level, the demand for an **`Sp`-equivariant splitting**
`V = 𝔽₂^{2n} = V_code ⊕ V_noise` on which `g` acts on `V_code` and fixes `V_noise`.
Such a splitting exists iff `V` has a proper nonzero `Sp`-invariant subspace.

## 2. Proposition (the finite Stone–von Neumann rigidity)

**Proposition.** `V = 𝔽₂^{2n}` is an **irreducible** `Sp(2n,𝔽₂)`-module: its only
invariant subspaces are `{0}` and `V`.

*Proof.* `Sp(2n,𝔽₂)` is transitive on `V∖{0}` (generated by the symplectic
transvections `t_u : x ↦ x + Ω(x,u)u`; verified `n=2`: orbit `15/15`, `n=3`: `63/63`).
Any invariant subspace containing a nonzero vector contains its whole orbit, i.e. all of
`V∖{0}`, hence `V`. ∎

This is the finite-field shadow of **Stone–von Neumann**: the Heisenberg/Weyl
representation (the Pauli operators on `V`) is irreducible, and the Weil/metaplectic
action of `Sp` intertwines it without reducing it. *The symplectic structure does not
split.*

## 3. Adjudication

**(i) Subspace-level decoupling is CLOSED — the obstruction is named.**
By the Proposition there is **no** `Sp`-equivariant `V_code ⊕ V_noise`. Hence one cannot
apply a code-randomizing symplectic element while holding a noise subspace fixed: every
`g ∈ Sp` acts on *all* of `V`, and the entangling elements (which transitivity forces
one to use) change the qubit-support of the error and so destroy the per-qubit noise
(handoff Prop. 4a). **The Weil action does not factor into code × noise.** This is the
**code-side mirror of the noise-side rigidity** `g(0)=2^{-n}`: both are one fact — the
symplectic structure is *rigid* (irreducible, non-splitting), so neither the noise
(Fourier-fixed point at max entropy) nor the action (no invariant noise subspace) can be
tuned. The two folklore barriers and these two rigidities are a single Stone–von Neumann
phenomenon.

**(ii) Reduction-level decoupling is OPEN (≈0), not BROKEN.**
The Proposition closes the *naive* "rotate the code, hold the noise" route. It does
**not** close a worst→avg reduction in general, because a Regev-style reduction never
rotates the worst-case error: it **adds fresh noise** to encode the worst-case instance
into average-case samples. Subspace-irreducibility says nothing about whether a
fresh-noise *encoding* of worst-case stabilizer decoding into average-case sympLPN
exists. That remains the open question — and it is `≈0` in-house (it is essentially the
construction the LSN papers report as facing "exotic" requirements).

**Verdict: OPEN.** The symmetry/Weil *decoupling* route is closed at the structural
(subspace) level by `Sp`-irreducibility = the finite Stone–von Neumann rigidity; a
reduction-level fresh-noise encoding is neither supplied nor excluded here.
