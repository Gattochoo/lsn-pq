## Scope

This increment extends the `N=2048,K=256,L=8` Monte Carlo from 200 trials to 2000 trials
at both paper design noise points:

- `p'=0.0706` for the `r=7` setting;
- `p'=0.0343` for the `r=11` setting.

The crate now also reports a simple one-sided 95% upper confidence bound for zero-error runs:

```text
upper = 1 - alpha^(1/trials), alpha = 0.05
```

For `trials=2000`, this is `0.0014967449`.

## Interpretation

This strengthens the empirical validation of the `N=2048` target from `0/200` to `0/2000`.
It is still nowhere near a direct empirical validation of the `2^-80` or `2^-128` design claims.

The honest statement is:

- the target length has now been exercised for 2000 independent trials at both design noise points;
- no block errors were observed;
- under the naive binomial model, a zero-error one-sided 95% upper bound is about `1.5e-3`.

This should update the paper limitation as empirical support, not remove the limitation.

## Next Step

Further tightening by brute-force Monte Carlo has diminishing returns. Reaching even `1e-6`
would require millions of trials. The next useful P1b path is importance sampling or fault-focused
stress testing; otherwise Codex should move to P2 scale cryptanalysis.
