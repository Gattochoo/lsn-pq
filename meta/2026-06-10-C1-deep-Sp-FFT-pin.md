## Source

**Paper:** C. de Concini and C. Procesi, *A characteristic free approach to invariant theory*, Advances in Mathematics **21** (1976), 330–354.

**Key section:** §6 (symplectic group), pages 349–352 of the PDF.

**Exact quote (via Domokos 2003, arXiv:math/0303106, Theorem 3.1 proof):**
> "the first fundamental theorem for the symplectic group Sp(2ν), which holds in its usual form in any characteristic (including 2), as was proved in [2, Section 6]."

**What §6 proves:** The algebra of Sp(2ν)-invariant polynomial functions on p copies of the standard module W = K^{2ν} is generated by the **pairwise symplectic inner products**
\[ B^{(ij)}(w^{(1)},\dotsc,w^{(p)}) = \langle w^{(i)}, w^{(j)} \rangle, \qquad 1 \le i \le j \le p, \]
where ⟨·,·⟩ is the fixed non-degenerate alternating form.

The phrase **"characteristic free"** in the title means the proof is valid over any field (including char 2). Domokos 2003 explicitly confirms this for char 2.

## Relevance to our paper

Our public quadratic constraints are exactly the **pairwise Ω-inner products** of the columns of the public matrix A:
\[ S_A = (a_i^T \Omega a_j)_{i,j=1}^{m}. \]

The FFT says that **S_A generates the full ring of Sp(2n)-invariant polynomial functions** of the public data. In other words:
- Any Sp(2n)-invariant feature of A is a polynomial in the entries of S_A.
- No additional invariant quantities exist beyond those captured by S_A.

This elevates the status of our Conjecture (source-level): if S_A = 0 characterizes the hardest LSN instances, then the FFT implies that **all symplectic invariants** of the public data vanish simultaneously at the hardest point. The conjecture is not about an arbitrary subset of invariants; it is about the **complete generating set**.

## Caveat (honest)

The FFT is for the **polynomial invariant ring** of the symplectic group action on the standard module. Our paper uses Sp(2n,F₂) over the finite field F₂, and the action is on the **set of isotropic matrices** (a subset of W^{⊕m}). The FFT for the full module implies the result for the isotropic subset (restriction of invariants), but the converse direction (whether new invariants appear on the subset) is not addressed by de Concini–Procesi. Over the finite field F₂, orbit-constant *functions* are strictly richer than polynomial invariants. The honest statement is:

> "By the characteristic-free FFT (de Concini–Procesi 1976 §6; valid in characteristic 2 [Domokos 2003, Thm 3.1 proof]), all *polynomial* invariants of the symplectic group on tuples of vectors are generated by the pairwise symplectic products — i.e., S_A exhausts the polynomial-invariant content of the public matrix. Whether additional invariants live on the isotropic locus, and the richer set-theoretic invariants over the finite field, are not controlled by the FFT; we use it as motivation only."
